    • MANGALYADHARANAM

      • The bride seats next to the groom on his left side; as close as possible to his heart. It is in this position that the groom performs the Mangalyadharanam, which involves lacing the sacred thread around his brideâ€™s neck. He then places red vermillion, locally called sindoor or kumkum, on the center of her forehead. The actions have the double significance of the groom declaring his life-time devotion to the bride while the red vermillion marks the bride as being promoted to the social status of being a wife.

The bride and the groom proceeded to put it on each other's heads, while the purohit chanted mantras from the Vedas. This was followed by a loud round of happy cheering from the guests.
